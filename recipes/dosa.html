Description

Dosa is a thin and crispy South Indian pancake made from a fermented batter of rice and lentils. It is light, flavorful, and commonly served with chutney and sambar, making it a popular breakfast or snack option.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup rice
  • 1/2 cup urad dal (black gram)
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed
  • Oil for cooking

Steps

  1. Soak rice and urad dal separately for 6 to 8 hours.
  2. Grind them into a smooth batter and mix together.
  3. Let the batter ferment overnight.
  4. Heat a pan and pour a ladle of batter, spreading it thin.
  5. Drizzle oil and cook until crispy and golden.
  6. Serve hot with chutney or sambar.
